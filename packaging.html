When designing Paul Randâ€™s Design and the Play Instinct, I wanted to focus on what recent graduates might like to see after entering the work force. The pink pop of color claims the attention of the viewer to draw them in and to make the design a bit more playful. The black and white is there to illustrate professionalism as an underlying base concept.
Through out my design, I relied heavily on vertical and horizontal relationships. I began to emphasize those relationships with rectangular elements and connecting an image or type across the pages.
Having a strong and refined structure is meant to project the confidence a graduate should remember to have when first designing in the real world.
